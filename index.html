Welcome to the home page of Meera Desai

About me:

I am a fourth year Ph.D. candidate in the School of Information at the University of Michigan where I am advised by Abigail Jacobs and Dallas Card. I am an affiliate of the Center for Ethics, Society, and Computing. Broadly, I am interested in the responsible development and use of natural language processing systems. You can view my CV here, check out my Google Scholar here, follow me on Twitter @MeeraDesai18 or Bluesky @madesai.bsky.social or email me at madesai [at] umich [dot] edu.

My work focuses on responsible data and evaluation practices for large language models and AI systems, using the lenses of measurement and construct valididty.

Research:

  • Wallach, Hanna, Meera Desai, A. Feder Cooper, Angelina Wang, Chat Atalla, Solon Barocas, Su Lin Blodgett, Alexandra Chouldechova et al. "Position: Evaluating Generative AI Systems is a Social Science Measurement Challenge." Forthcoming at ICML (2025). [link]

Before Michigan:

I graduated from Barnard College in 2019 with a B.A. in physics and a minor in computer science. After Barnard and before starting my Ph.D., I spent two years at the American Museum of Natural History in New York as a computational research fellow, where I worked on an applied machine learning project improving short term prediction of small-scale ocean current features. At the museum, I also taught introductory coding and oceanography to two groups of incredible high school students.
