Welcome to the home page of Meera Desai

About me:

I am a fifth year Ph.D. candidate in the School of Information at the University of Michigan where I am advised by Abigail Jacobs and Dallas Card. I am an affiliate of the Center for Ethics, Society, and Computing. This past spring, I interned at Microsoft Research New York at the FATE group, where I had the best time working with Hanna Wallach and Alex Chouldechova.

My research focuses on responsible data and evaluation practices for generative AI systems, using measurement and construct validity as a lens. You can view my CV here, check out my Google Scholar here, follow me on Twitter @MeeraDesai18 or Bluesky @madesai.bsky.social or email me at madesai@umich.edu.

Before Michigan:

I graduated from Barnard College in 2019 with a B.A. in physics and a minor in computer science. After Barnard and before starting my Ph.D., I spent two years at the American Museum of Natural History in New York as a computational research fellow, where I worked on an applied machine learning project improving short term prediction of small-scale ocean current features. At the museum, I also taught introductory coding and oceanography to two groups of incredible high school students.
